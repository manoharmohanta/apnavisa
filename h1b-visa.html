H1B USA Visa

The H-1B visa is a non-immigrant visa category in the United States that allows U.S. employers to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations.

Here are some key points about the H-1B visa

Specialty Occupation: The H-1B visa is designed for jobs that require specialized knowledge and a bachelor's degree or higher (or its equivalent) in a specific field. Examples include engineering, mathematics, computer science, medicine, and many others

Employer Sponsorship: To obtain an H-1B visa, a U.S. employer must sponsor the foreign worker by petitioning for them with the United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS). The employer must demonstrate that the position requires specialized skills and that they were unable to find a qualified U.S. worker for the job.

Duration of Stay: Initially, H-1B visas are granted for up to three years, with the possibility of extension for an additional three years, for a maximum total of six years. Under certain circumstances, H-1B holders may be eligible for further extensions beyond the six-year limit.

Dual Intent: H-1B visa holders are allowed to pursue lawful permanent residence (a green card) while maintaining their H-1B status. This is known as "dual intent," meaning they can have both temporary non-immigrant intent (H-1B) and immigrant intent (green card application) simultaneously.

Quota and Lottery System: There is an annual cap on the number of new H-1B visas issued each fiscal year. Currently, this cap is set at 85,000, with 65,000 visas allocated for individuals with bachelor's degrees or equivalent, and an additional 20,000 visas reserved for those with advanced degrees (master's or higher) from U.S. universities. When the number of applications exceeds the available visas, USCIS conducts a random lottery to select the beneficiaries.

Dependents: H-1B visa holders' spouses and unmarried children under 21 years of age are eligible for H-4 visas, allowing them to accompany the H-1B holder to the United States. H-4 visa holders may also apply for work authorization under certain circumstances.
