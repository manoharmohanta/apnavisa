USA Visiting Visa

B1 Visa

This is a non-immigrant visa for individuals traveling to the United States temporarily for business purposes. It allows holders to engage in activities such as attending business meetings, conferences, negotiations, or other professional events. B1 visa holders are not permitted to work or receive any form of compensation from a U.S. source during their stay.

B2 Visa

This is a non-immigrant visa for individuals traveling to the United States temporarily for tourism, pleasure, or visiting friends and relatives. It allows holders to engage in activities such as tourism, vacation, social visits, amateur participation in musical, sports, or similar events, and medical treatment. Like the B1 visa, B2 visa holders are not allowed to work in the United States.

B1 and B2 visas

The B1 and B2 visas are often issued together as a B1/B2 visa, allowing holders to engage in both business and tourist activities during their stay in the U.S. The duration of stay granted with a B1/B2 visa can vary, but it's typically limited to a maximum of six months per visit.

B-1 / B-2 Checklist

Personal Information:
  • Passport: Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your intended period of stay in the United States.
  • Passport-sized photos (Only For Drop Box): Have recent, colour passport-sized photographs meeting the specified requirements.
  • Complete DS-160 form: Fill out the DS-160 Non-immigrant Visa Application Form online and print the confirmation page with the barcode.
  • Interview confirmation: Bring the confirmation page with the barcode for your interview appointment.
Financial Documentation:
  • Bank statements: Provide recent bank statements to demonstrate your ability to cover expenses during your stay.
  • Proof of employment: Present an employment letter or pay stubs to verify your employment status.
  • Income tax returns: Include copies of income tax returns for the past few years to prove financial stability.
Travel Itinerary:
  • Round-trip ticket: Show proof of booked round-trip airline tickets or a travel itinerary.
  • Accommodation details: Provide information about where you will stay during your visit, such as hotel reservations or a letter of invitation from a host.
Purpose of Visit:
  • Business itinerary: If traveling for business (B1 visa), provide details of meetings, conferences, or training sessions you plan to attend.
  • Tourist itinerary: If traveling for tourism (B2 visa), outline your planned activities and destinations during your stay.
Additional Documentation:
  • Previous visas: If you've had visas to other countries, especially the United States, provide copies of those visas.
  • Travel history: Prepare a summary of your travel history, including previous visits to the United States if applicable.
