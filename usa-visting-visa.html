B1 Visa

This is a non-immigrant visa for individuals traveling to the United States temporarily for business purposes. It allows holders to engage in activities such as attending business meetings, conferences, negotiations, or other professional events. B1 visa holders are not permitted to work or receive any form of compensation from a U.S. source during their stay.

B2 Visa

This is a non-immigrant visa for individuals traveling to the United States temporarily for tourism, pleasure, or visiting friends and relatives. It allows holders to engage in activities such as tourism, vacation, social visits, amateur participation in musical, sports, or similar events, and medical treatment. Like the B1 visa, B2 visa holders are not allowed to work in the United States.

The B1 and B2 visas are often issued together as a B1/B2 visa, allowing holders to engage in both business and tourist activities during their stay in the U.S. The duration of stay granted with a B1/B2 visa can vary, but it's typically limited to a maximum of six months per visit.

It's essential to note that while these visas allow entry into the United States for temporary purposes, they do not grant the holder the right to live or work in the country permanently. Additionally, obtaining a B1/B2 visa requires applicants to demonstrate strong ties to their home country, such as stable employment, family, and financial assets, to prove their intent to return after their temporary stay in the United States.

The duration of a B1/B2 visa for the United States varies depending on several factors, including the discretion of the consular officer issuing the visa and the purpose of the visit. Here are some key points regarding the duration:

  1. Validity Period: The validity period of a B1/B2 visa is typically determined by the consular officer and can vary from a few months to up to ten years. During this validity period, the visa holder can enter the United States multiple times, as long as the visa remains valid and the purpose of each visit aligns with the visa category (business, tourism, or both).

  2. Duration of Each Stay: While the visa itself may be valid for a more extended period, the duration of each stay in the United States is typically limited to a maximum of six months. However, the specific length of stay granted is determined by the U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officer at the port of entry. They have the authority to admit visitors for a shorter period, depending on the purpose of the visit and other factors.

  3. Extension of Stay: In some cases, individuals in the United States on a B1/B2 visa may be eligible to apply for an extension of their stay if they have a valid reason, such as medical treatment or unforeseen circumstances preventing their departure. However, extensions are granted at the discretion of the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) and are not guaranteed.

  4. Dual Intent: It's important to note that while the B1/B2 visa is primarily for temporary visits, individuals can still have dual intent, meaning they may intend to visit temporarily while also having an underlying intent to immigrate to the United States in the future. However, they must be able to demonstrate their intent to return to their home country at the end of each visit to comply with the terms of the visa.
