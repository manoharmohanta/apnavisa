Here are some points regarding the F1 and F2 visas

F-1 Visa Purpose: The F-1 visa is specifically for students who intend to enrol in a full-time academic program or English language program at a U.S. college, university, seminary, conservatory, academic high school, elementary school, or other academic institution. It allows individuals to enter the United States for the primary purpose of studying.

F-2 Visa Purpose: The F-2 visa is designed for the dependents of F-1 visa holders who are pursuing academic studies or language training programs in the United States. Dependents eligible for an F-2 visa include the spouse and unmarried children under the age of 21 of the F-1 visa holder.

F1 Visa Duration for STEM Programs: F1 students who graduate with degrees in certain STEM fields may be eligible for a 24-month Additional Optional Practical Training (OPT) extension, allowing them to work in the United States for up to three years after completing their degree.

F1 Visa Duration for Non-STEM Programs: F1 students who graduate with degrees in certain non-STEM students are only eligible for the standard 12-month OPT period after completing their degree. allowing them to work in the United States for only one years after completing their degree.

F2 Visa Duration of Stay: F2 visa holders are admitted to the United States for the same duration as the F1 visa holder's program of study. Therefore, the duration of stay for F2 visa holders depends on the length of the F1 student's academic program.

If the F1 student is enrolled in a STEM program and is eligible for the STEM OPT extension, the F2 visa holder may also benefit from the extended period if they remain in the United States during the OPT period.

Maintaining Status: F1 visa holders must maintain full-time enrolment at their educational institution and comply with all visa regulations to remain in legal status. F2 visa holders must also abide by visa regulations and are dependent on the F1 visa holder's compliance with their academic program requirements.

Employment: F1 visa holders may be eligible for on-campus employment and practical training opportunities under certain conditions. How ever F2 visa holders are not eligible for employment in the United States, but they may engage in volunteer activities and pursue recreational interests.
