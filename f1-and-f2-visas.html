F1 and F2 visas

F1 Visa

This is a non-immigrant visa for individuals traveling to the United States temporarily for business purposes. It allows holders to engage in activities such as attending business meetings, conferences, negotiations, or other professional events. B1 visa holders are not permitted to work or receive any form of compensation from a U.S. source during their stay.

F2 Visa

This is a non-immigrant visa for individuals traveling to the United States temporarily for tourism, pleasure, or visiting friends and relatives. It allows holders to engage in activities such as tourism, vacation, social visits, amateur participation in musical, sports, or similar events, and medical treatment. Like the B1 visa, B2 visa holders are not allowed to work in the United States.

F1 Visa Checklist

  • Passport (current and all the previous ones)
  • I-20
  • Visa Interview Appointment Letter
  • DS 160 Confirmation
  • SEVIS Fee Receipt
  • Academic transcripts like Class 10th and 12th Certificates and Bachelors
  • GRE Score Card If Any
  • IELTS or TOEFL scorecards or even PTE scorecards
  • Work Experience Letters (Form 16 / Salary Slips at least 3 Months / Appointment Letter / Relieving Letter / Any Internship Certificates )
  • Loan Approval Letter (if applicable)
  • Saving Bank Account Statement (recommended: last 6 Months Statement)
  • Fixed Deposit Certificates
  • Parents Tax Returns for the last two years.
  • Other Admits if any.

F2 Visa Checklist

  • DS-160 Confirmation Page
  • Passport (Current and Old)
  • Photographs (In case of Drop Box Appointment)
  • Relationship Documentation
  • F-2 I20
  • F-1 visa holder Documents (F-1 I20,Visa Stamping and Passport )
  • Visa Interview Appointment Confirmation
  • Any Additional Required Documents
  • Proof of Financial Support: Show evidence that the F-1 visa holder can financially support their dependents during their stay in the United States. This may include bank statements, financial aid award letters, or other financial documentation.
