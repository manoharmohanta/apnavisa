Types of USA Visas

Visitor Visa (B-1/B-2): This visa is for temporary visitors for either business (B-1) or tourism (B-2). The B-1 visa allows individuals to engage in business activities such as meetings, conferences, and negotiations, while the B-2 visa is for tourism, vacations, or medical treatment.

Student Visas (F-1/M-1): These visas are for individuals who want to pursue academic or vocational studies in the United States. The F-1 visa is for academic students, while the M-1 visa is for vocational students.

H-1B Visa: For individuals in specialty occupations that require theoretical or technical expertise.

L-1 Visa: For intracompany transferees who work for a multinational company and are being transferred to a U.S. branch, parent, subsidiary, or affiliate. .

O Visa: For individuals with extraordinary ability or achievement in fields such as science, arts, education, business, or athletics

TN Visa: For Canadian and Mexican citizens working in certain professional occupations under the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA).

Exchange Visitor Visa (J-1): This visa is for individuals participating in approved exchange programs, including students, scholars, professors, research assistants, and professionals.

Investor Visa (EB-5): The EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program grants lawful permanent residence to foreign investors who invest a certain amount of capital in a new commercial enterprise that creates jobs for U.S. workers.

Diplomatic and Official Visas: These visas are for government officials, diplomats, and employees of international organizations traveling to the U.S. on official business.

Transit Visa (C): For individuals passing through the U.S. en route to another country.

Religious Worker Visa (R-1): For individuals coming to the U.S. to work in a religious capacity for a qualified religious organization.
